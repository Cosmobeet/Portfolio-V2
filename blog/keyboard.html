Mechanical Keyboard Hobby

March 24, 2022

How i got started

Like many others, covid was the perfect time to pick up another hobby. I had always been an avid computer user, so getting into mechanical keyboards seemed like a logical choice. I started out with a generic 60%(Excludes function keys and numpad) mechanical keyboard i had found on amazon. Overall for about $60-70 usd i was pretty happy with my purchase, it was a compact keyboard with gateron brown switches, bluetooth, and rgb backlit keys. Link

Customization

This is usually where the appeal is for most people, having control over how your keyboard looks, feels, and sounds. This can involve replacing or modifying parts of the keyboard such as keycaps, switches, and stabilizers.

Group buy mechanical keyboards

This is where a lot of my friends think i've gone too far down the rabbit hole. Group buy mechanical keyboards are usually a lot more expensive than typical mechanical keyboards, they range anywhere from $200 to $600, and are usually even more if you're buying second-hand because of the limited supply.

Manufacturing parts itself can be incredibily expensive, group buys are a process to collect enough interest and buying power to secure manufacturers at a lower price point through mass production. This entire process including production and assembly can often take anywhere from a few months to over a year.
